Ethnic Diversity

Nigeria is home to over 250 ethnic groups, including the Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo, Fulani, and Tiv. Each group has its own language, customs, and traditions that contribute to the nationâ€™s vibrant cultural mosaic.

Traditional Festivals

  • Argungu Fishing Festival :Celebrated in Kebbi State with canoe races and fishing competitions.
  • Osun-Osogbo Festival :A spiritual celebration honoring the river goddess Osun in Osogbo.
  • Durbar Festival :A royal parade of horses and warriors in northern Nigeria.
  • New Yam Festival :Celebrated by Igbo communities to mark the harvest season.

Music & Dance

Nigerian music is globally recognized, from Afrobeat pioneered by Fela Kuti to traditional genres like Fuji, Highlife, and Igbo drumming. Dances like Atilogwu and Bata express joy, storytelling, and spirituality.

Food & Cuisine

Nigerian cuisine is rich and flavorful. Popular dishes include jollof rice, pounded yam with egusi soup etc.Each region offers unique culinary delights.
